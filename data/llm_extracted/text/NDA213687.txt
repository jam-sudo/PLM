5.1. Nonclinical Assessment of Potential  
Effectiveness  
The nonclinical assessment characterizing the effectiveness of triheptanoin (UX007) in LC­
FAOD comes from published pharmacodynamic (PD) studies using tissues from in vitro and in 
vivo animal models. 
•  In excised rat liver perfused with 13C-labeled 1mM heptanoate, it was demonstrated that 
for every molecule of heptanoate (metabolite of triheptanoin) uptake by the liver, two 
molecules of acetyl-CoA and one molecule of propionyl-CoA are produced. The acetyl-
CoA enters the TCA cycle where it is used for adenosine triphosphate production and 
ketogenesis (Deng et al. 2009). The effects of a single IV infusion of 13C heptanoate were 
studied in the brains of wildtype mice and compared to those of glucose transporter 1 
(GLUT-1)–deficient transgenic mice. Although glucose concentrations were significantly 
elevated in both wildtype and GLUT-1 knockout (KO) mice following heptanoate 
infusion, the brain levels of acetyl CoA were higher in the GLUT-1 KO animals than in 
the wildtype mice. Similarly, although the neurotransmitter glutamine levels were higher 
than the glutamate levels in the brain of both animals, the glutamine levels were 
significantly higher in the KO animal brain than in the wildtype animal brain, indicating a 
potential compensatory mechanism by reactive astrocytes to the lack of GLUT-1 protein. 
The evidence of labeled substrate and metabolites in both the liver and brain suggests that 
heptanoate can diffuse across the blood brain barrier and plasma membrane to produce 
energy for all TCA cycle-dependent cell types (Marin-Valencia et al. 2013).

14.1.1. Population PK Analysis 
Pharmacokinetic data from aforementioned clinical studies showed a large between-subject 
variability. Also note that plasma heptanoate concentrations do not show a typical linear 
absorption and elimination kinetics after oral administration of triheptanoin. The Applicant’s 
population PK analysis (Population PK Study Report, ULTR-PMX-UX007-852) is limited by 
insufficient PK data due to sparse PK sampling in a small number of patients with LC-FAOD, 
and may not be adequate to describe PK of heptanoate. Therefore, PK parameters in study 
UX007-CL101, estimated by a noncompartmental method, are used to inform PK of triheptanoin 
in the label (see Section II.5).

14.2. In Vitro Studies 
Protein Biding Studies 
Study UX007-PC020: Protein Binding of Heptanoate 
In the human plasma, heptanoate exhibited moderately high human plasma protein binding 
(~80% at the range of 40μM to 400μM). 
Study UX007-PC018: Protein Binding of Valproic Acid in the Presence of Heptanoate 
In the presence of 400μM heptanoate, the unbound fraction of valproic acid increased about 
twofold, suggesting that heptanoate may competitively displace valproic acid from binding to 
plasma proteins. Presence of valproic acid did not affect the protein binding of heptanoate.

Metabolism 
Study UX007-PC015: Lipase-Mediated Metabolism 
The in vitro evaluation of lipase-mediated metabolism showed that triheptanoin was hydrolyzed 
by three lipases tested (pancreatic triglyceride lipase, pancreatic triglyceride lipase-related 
protein 2, and carboxyl ester lipase). 
Study UX007-PC023: Metabolic Stability of Heptanoate in Human Hepatocytes 
In vitro metabolism of heptanoate was investigated using human hepatocytes. The mean half-life 
of heptanoate was estimated to be 28 minutes in the human hepatocytes. The in vitro-in vivo 
scaled hepatic clearance of heptanoate in humans was estimated to be 18.5 mL/min/kg, 
corresponding to ~90% of human hepatic blood flow. 
Study UX007-PC019: CYP and UGT-Mediated Metabolism 
Based on the in vitro evaluation in the human microsomes, neither heptanoate nor BHP was a 
sensitive substrate of human CYP or UGT enzymes. 
Study UX007-PC025: Identification of Metabolites in Human Liver Microsomes 
In vitro metabolites of heptanoate from human liver microsomes, no oxidation metabolites of 
heptanoate were detected, indicating that heptanoate is not a sensitive substrate of CYP enzymes. 
A direct glucuronide conjugate of heptanoate was observed, but the amount of this metabolite 
was negligible (~0.08 ppm). 
Drug-Drug Interactions 
Study UX007-PC021: Potential CYP Inhibition 
The potential for heptanoate or BHP to elicit drug-drug interactions via an inhibition of CYPs 
was investigated in an in vitro study. At 40 and 400µM of heptanoate or 10 and 50µM of BHP, 
neither heptanoate nor BHP showed appreciable inhibition of the major human CYP isoforms 
tested: CYP3A4, CYP1A2, CYP2B6, CYP2C8, CYP2C9, CYP2C19, and CYP2D6. 
14.3. Bioanalysis 
In study UX007-CL101, plasma concentrations of triheptanoin and its three metabolites 
(heptanoate, BHB, and BHP) were measured using validated bioanalytical methods with LC­
MS/MS. Method validations were adequate to support the study UX007-CL101. 
The performance and validation parameters for plasma concentrations of heptanoate, BHB, and

triheptanoin concentrations are not included in this review because most concentrations of
