5 Nonclinical Pharmacology/Toxicology 
Executive Summary 
The nonclinical studies for tivozanib hydrochloride were reviewed on May 24, 2013 under NDA 
# 204408 (Applicant-owned) for the the treatment of patients with advanced renal cell 
carcinoma (RCC). See Appendix 19.3. In the current executive summary,  risk estimates are 
calculated based on the maximum recommended human dose of 1.34 mg/day for adult 
patients with relapsed or refractory advanced RCC. 
 
Tivozanib hydrochloride is an anticancer agent of the Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor 
Receptor (VEGFR) Tyrosine Kinase inhibitor class and is being developed for the treatment of 
advanced renal cell carcinoma.  In vitro cellular kinase assays demonstrated that tivozanib is an 
inhibitor of VEGF receptors -1, -2, and -3 (IC50 0.107, 0.081, and 0.122 ng/mL, respectively) and

IC50 of these kinases is well below the Cmin value achieved in RCC patients (16 to 30.9 ng/mL) 
following repeat dosing at the recommended dose at 1.34 mg/day, suggesting continuous 
inhibition of these kinases throughout the treatment period.   
 
In vivo pharmacodynamic studies in mice and rats bearing human or murine xenograft tumors 
demonstrated that tivozanib inhibited tumor growth at a dose of 0.2 mg/kg in rats, via 
inhibition of angiogenesis and vascular permeability. This dose is associated with plasma levels 
of tivozanib in rodents (Cmax of 70 - 108 ng/ml; AUC of 1640 ng*hr/ml), which were within range 
of the Cmax and AUC achieved in patients with solid tumors following repeat dosing at the 
recommended dose of 1. 34 mg/day  (Cmax was - 86.9 ng/mL; AUC0-24 was  1510 ng*hr/mL).  
 
The selection of animal models to be used in toxicology and safety pharmacology studies (rats 
and monkeys) was based on PK and/or metabolic profiles of tivozanib. The oral route was 
utilized in nonclinical studies as this is the intended clinical route of exposure. The tivozanib 
toxicokinetic profile following repeat oral administration was approximately linear in rats and 
monkeys, with Cmax and AUC increasing in an approximately dose proportional manner.  No 
gender differences were shown in the rat or monkey.  Interspecies differences in the rate of 
absorption (Tmax) and elimination half-life (t1/2) were reported. The elimination t1/2 in rats (9-45 
hours) was greater than that reported in monkeys (8-12 hours), but was considerably shorter

Pharmacology and Clinical Pharmacokinetics

following both single dose and repeated doses. At the recommended dosage of 1.34 mg QD,

approximately 6- to 7- fold the exposure observed at single-dose levels.

General Dosing and Therapeutic Individualization 
General Dosing 
The proposed dosing regimen is 1.34 mg oral once daily (QD) for 3 weeks on and 1 week off, 
with or without food.  
 
Dose Selection Rationale and Dose- and Exposure-Response Relationships  
The doses of 0.89, 1.34, and 1.78 mg were evaluated in the first-in-human dose escalation study 
(Study AV-951-03-B01). The selection of the proposed dose was supported by dose-dependent 
effect of tivozanib on dose-limiting toxicity and hypertension in the first-in-human study, and 
also supported by pharmacodynamic analysis, which demonstrated dose-dependent increases 
in serum VEGF-A levels, dose-dependent decreases in serum soluble VEGFR-2 levels and a dose-
dependent reduction in tumor perfusion. Due to the incidence of dose-limiting toxicities (DLTs)  
at the highest dose level tested (1.78 mg), the 1.34 mg dose of tivozanib was identified as the 
highest dose level with an acceptable safety/tolerability profile.  
With regard to the on/off period, the first-in-human study was initiated with a 4 weeks on/2 
weeks off schedule, and then altered to a 3 weeks on/1 week off schedule due to the following 
reasons: 1) Adverse events in FIH Study AV-951-03-B01 were rapidly reversible upon stopping 
treatment, supporting a shorter rest period; 2) Nonclinical studies showed that tumors regrew 
after cessation of tivozanib. 
 
The proposed dosing regimen (1.34 mg oral once daily (QD) for 3 weeks on and 1 week off, with 
or without food) was subsequently studied in four studies: AV-951-07-201, AV-951-10-202, AV-
951-09-301 (TIVO-1), AV-951-15-303 (TIVO-3). The primary evidence of efficacy and safety is 
provided by pivotal, randomized, open-label, controlled trial (TIVO-3) in patients with relapsed 
or refractory advanced RCC, who received prior systemic treatments (2 TKIs, a TKI plus a 
checkpoint inhibitor or a TKI plus other agent). Safety data included 1008 RCC patients treated 
with FOTIVDA who were enrolled in 6 monotherapy studies that included patients from the 
pivotal randomized study TIVO-3 comparing tivozanib to sorafenib. The proposed dosage is 
effective and has a manageable safety profile.

Specific Safety Studies/Clinical Trials 
The sponsor conducted a hepatic impairment study (AV-951-12-118) between in hepatically 
impaired patients and matched normal controls, both without cancer, using the Child-Pugh 
categories of hepatic impairment, between August 2012 and September 2013. After a 10-hour 
fast, 44 patients received 1 dose of 1.5 mg tivozanib hydrochloride. Pharmacokinetic results are 
described in Section 19.4 (OCP Appendices).  
 
Overall, 11/22 (50%) of patients with hepatic impairment and 5/22 (23%) of patients with 
normal hepatic function at baseline had TEAEs. The highest grade TEAEs were two grade 3 AEs 
of hypertension, one in a patient with moderate hepatic impairment and one in a patient with 
severe hepatic impairment. Hypertension was also the most common TEAE in the entire study, 
occurring in 25-37.5% (of 8 patients enrolled in each of the mild and moderate hepatic 
impairment groups, and 6 patients in the severe hepatic impairment group) of patients with 
mild, moderate, or severe hepatic impairment, and in 23% of patients with normal hepatic 
function. 
 
In conjunction with the pharmacokinetic results, these findings support the recommendation in 
labeling to adjust the dosage in patients with moderate hepatic impairment and avoid use in 
patients with severe hepatic impairment.

1   Executive Summary 
 
1.1   Introduction 
NDA 204408 for tivozanib was submitted to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration as a New 
Drug Application (NDA) for the indication of the treatment of patients with advanced renal cell 
carcinoma (RCC). Tivozanib is a new molecular entity kinase inhibitor, which inhibits multiple 
kinases including vascular endothelial growth factor receptor (VEGFR)-1, VEGFR-2, and VEGFR-
3. The recommended maximum human dose is 1.5 mg taken orally once daily, on a schedule of 
3 weeks-on treatment followed by 1 week-off. Nonclinical pharmacology, pharmacokinetic and 
toxicology studies have been submitted to support the approval of tivozanib for the proposed 
indication.  
 
1.2  Brief Discussion of Nonclinical Findings 
 
Tivozanib hydrochloride is an anticancer agent of the Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor 
Receptor (VEGFR) Tyrosine Kinase inhibitor class and is being developed for the treatment of 
advanced renal cell carcinoma.  In vitro cellular kinase assays demonstrated that tivozanib is an 
inhibitor of VEGF receptors -1, -2, and -3 (IC50 0.107, 0.081, and 0.122 ng/mL, respectively) and 
other kinases such as c-KЕқЖҡ ДӮЕ¶Дҡ W'&ZЙҙ ДӮЖҡ ЕҡЕқЕҗЕҡДһЖҢ /50вҖҷs (0.83 and 0.88 ng/ml, respectively).  The 
IC50 of these kinases is well below the Cmin value achieved in RCC patients (16 to 30.9 ng/mL) 
following repeat dosing at the recommended dose at 1.5 mg/day, suggesting continuous 
inhibition of these kinases throughout the treatment period.   
 
In vivo pharmacodynamic studies in mice and rats bearing human or murine xenograft tumors 
demonstrated that tivozanib inhibited tumor growth at a dose of 0.2 mg/kg in rats, via 
inhibition of angiogenesis and vascular permeability. This dose is associated with a plasma 
levels of tivozanib in rodents (Cmax of 70 - 108 ng/ml; AUC of 1640 ng*hr/ml), which were within 
range of the Cmax and AUC achieved in RCC patients following repeat dosing at the 
recommended dose of 1.5 mg/day (Cmax was 67.5 - 94.3 ng/mL; AUC0-24 was 1180 -1641 
ng*hr/mL).  
 
The selection of animal models to be used in toxicology and safety pharmacology studies (rats 
and monkeys) was based on PK and/or metabolic profiles of tivozanib. The oral route was 
utilized in nonclinical studies as this is the intended clinical route of exposure. The tivozanib 
toxicokinetic profile following repeat oral administration was approximately linear in rats and 
monkeys, with Cmax and AUC increasing in an approximately dose proportional manner.  No 
gender differences were shown in the rat or monkey.  Interspecies differences in the rate of
