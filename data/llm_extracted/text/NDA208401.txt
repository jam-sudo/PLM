2.0 QUESTION BASED REVIEW
The clinical pharmacology aspects of lisinopril have previously been described in detail for 
Zestril® under NDA 19777.  Hence, an abbreviated version of the question based review is 
presented here to address the clinical pharmacology issues pertinent to this submission.  
2.1 What are the proposed dosages and routes of administration?
The route of administration is oral. The proposed label for lisinopril oral solution and the label of 
Zestril® tablets 
. For the treatment of hypertension in adults the recommended initial 
dose is 10 mg once-daily. The dosage should be adjusted according to blood pressure response. 
In pediatric patients in the age ≥ 6 years and GFR > 30 mL/min/1.73m2, the recommended 
starting dose is 0.07 mg/kg once daily (up to 5 mg once daily). Dosage should be adjusted 
according to blood pressure response up to a maximum of 0.61 mg/kg (up to 40 mg daily).
For the treatment of heart failure, when used with diuretics (and usually digitalis) as adjunctive 
therapy for systolic heart failure, the initial dose is 5 mg once-daily. The recommended starting 
dose in these patients with hyponatremia (serum sodium < 130 mL/min) is 2.5 mg once-daily 
and increased as tolerated to a maximum of 40 mg once-daily.                                                                                 
For the reduction of mortality in acute myocardial heart infarction the recommended dose is 5 
mg orally followed by 5 mg after 24 h, 
 and then 10 mg once-daily. Dosing 
should continue for at least six weeks. 
2.2 What are the design features of the clinical pharmacology and the clinical studies used 
to support dosing or claims?
The applicant performed two randomized crossover bioequivalence studies with lisinopril oral 
solution (10 mL x 1 mg/mL) and Zestril® tablets (10 mg) in healthy subjects in the fasted and fed 
state. 
2.3 What is the relative bioavailability of lisinopril oral solution to Zestril® Tablets?
Following oral administration, lisinopril oral solution and Zestril® Tablets are bioequivalent.  
The geometric mean ratio for Cmax was 0.94 (90% CI: 0.83 – 1.06) and AUC0-inf was 0.94 (90% 
CI: 0.86 – 1.02) under fasted conditions.
2.4 What is the effect of food on the bioavailability of the drug from the dosage form?
Following the ingestion of an FDA standard high calorie, high fat breakfast, the systemic 
exposure to lisinopril for both the products is decreased by 30-40% (cross-study comparison 
between fasted vs fed bioequivalence studies).  Nevertheless, under fed condition, lisinopril oral 
solution and Zestril® Tablets are bioequivalent.  The geometric mean ratio for Cmax was 0.99 
(90% CI: 0.96 – 1.03) and AUC0-inf was 0.99 (90% CI: 0.96 – 1.03).  It should be noted that a 
minor negative food effect (10-20% decrease in lisinopril exposure) was also observed during 
the original NDA review for Zestril® tablets and deemed not clinically significant. The US 
Package Insert for Zestril® recommends dosing without regards to meals.

3.0 INDIVIDUAL STUDY REVIEWS
3.1 Fasted Bioequivalence Study (GS03-01)
          
Protocol: SG03-01
Study Period: 12/6-17, 2014
Title: A Randomized Single Dose, Two-Period, Two-Treatment, Two-Way Crossover Study to Determine the 
Relative Bioavailability of 10 mL of Lisinopril Oral Solution, 1 mg/mL, versus Zestril® 10 mg Tablets under 
Fasted Conditions in Healthy Adults
Study Design: Open-label, randomized, two-period, two-treatment, cross-over study in which 56 healthy adult 
subjects received a single 10 mL dose of lisinopril oral solution, 1 mg/mL, in one period and a single Zestril 
Lisinopril 10 mg tablet in another period, following an overnight fast of at least 10 h
 Bioequivalence
 Bioavailability
Background: Zestril (Lisinopril) is an oral long acting angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor indicated 
for the treatment of hypertension in adult patients and pediatric patients 6 years of age and older to lower blood 
pressure. Zestril is also indicated to reduce signs and symptoms of systolic heart failure and for reduction of 
mortality in treatment of hemodynamically stable patients within 24 h of acute myocardial infarction. 
Following oral administration of Zestril, peak serum concentrations of lisinopril occur within about 7 h after 
administration. Food does not alter the bioavailability of lisinopril after administration of Zestril. Declining serum 
concentrations exhibit a prolonged terminal phase which does not contribute to drug accumulation. The terminal 
phase probably represents saturable binding to ACE and is not proportional to dose. Upon multiple dose 
administration lisinopril exhibits an effective half-life of 12 h. Lisinopril is not bound to plasma proteins and is 
excreted unchanged entirely in urine. Based on urinary recovery, the mean extent of absorption is approximately 25 
(6-60) %. The absolute bioavailability of lisinopril is reduced to 16 % in patients with stable New York Heart 
Association (NYHA) Class II-IV congestive heart failure and the volume of distribution is slightly smaller than in 
healthy subjects. The absolute bioavailability of lisinopril in patients with acute myocardial infarction is similar to 
that in healthy subjects.
Commonly reported adverse events after administration of Zestril include cough, dizziness, drowsiness, headache, 
depressed mood, nausea, vomitus, diarrhea, upset stomach, mild itching and rash.
Rationale: There is currently no FDA approved, ready-to use oral liquid formulation of lisinopril for use by 
pediatric patients who require weight based dosing or older children and adults who have difficulty swallowing or 
cannot consume tablets. An oral solution of lisinopril would solve this problem.
Objectives: To compare the rate of absorption and oral bioavailability of a test formulation of 10 mL of lisinopril 
oral solution, 1 mg/mL (Silvergate), to an equivalent oral dose of the commercially available comparator product, 
Zestril 10 mg tablet (Astra-Zeneca), when administered under fasted conditions. The 10 mg dose is the initial dose 
recommended by the label for Zestril tablets.
Screening: Within 28 days prior to study start
Washout: ≥ 7  days
