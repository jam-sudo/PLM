When a Dexilant SoluTab 30 mg tablet was mixed with water and administered via an oral 
syringe or via an NG tube, or swallowed as an intact OD tablet with water, each of them were 
bioequivalent to the oral administration of the intact OD tablet on the tongue allowed to 
disintegrate and swallowed without water.  Therefore, in the label, we recommend that Dexilant 
SoluTab 30 mg can be administered with water via oral syringe or NG tube or may also be 
swallowed whole with water.
Disintegration Time:
In-vivo mean disintegration time for Dexilant SoluTab 30 mg tablet on tongue was 36 second 
(ranged 29-43 seconds).  However, in the study that measured disintegration time, subjects sipped 
and swallowed 20 mL of spring water 30 seconds prior to the study drug administration which 
could shorten the true disintegration time.  As the general definition of ODT is rapid oral 
disintegration in saliva without the need for chewing or drinking liquids to ingest these products, 
the reported in vivo disintegration time in this study with sipping 20 mL of water 30 seconds 
prior to the drug administration could be biased and unreliable.  Therefore, the reported 
disintegration time in this study will not have a labeling implication.  Nonetheless, the reported 
in-vivo disintegration time in this study is in a similar range as the reported in-vitro disintegration 
time of 32.9 second with 95% CI of 23.2-42.6 second. 
Alcohol Dose Dumping:
Based on the in-vitro alcohol dose dumping study, there is a significant potential of alcohol dose-
dumping at the acid stage in the presence of 40% alcohol.  90% of drug was released within 30 
min in the presence of 40% alcohol, compared to 1-2% of drug release at the same time point in 
the presence of 0%, 5%, and 20% alcohol.  This observed alcohol dose dumping may potentially 
shorten the tmax, reduce the AUC and Cmax of dexlansoprazole by exposing this acid labile drug 
early in the acidic environment of the stomach, and thus, may reduce the efficacy.  However, we 
do not anticipate the safety profile of the drug be affected by the alcohol dose dumping and a 
follow up in-vivo alcohol dose dumping study may not be needed.    This observed alcohol dose 
dumping issue would be addressed in the product labeling by instructing patients to “avoid 
alcohol when taking Dexilant SoluTab”. 
OSI Inspection:

390MR(OD)_105 (BE study comparing 30 mg Dexilant capsule vs. 30 mg Dexilant ODT) and 
for study TAK-390MR(OD)_104 (BE study comparing 60 mg Dexilant capsule vs. 2 X 30 mg 
Dexilant ODT) for both clinical sites and bioanalytical sites on 04/20/2015.  However, the 
Division of New Drug Bioequivalence Evaluation (DNDBE) within the Office of Study Integrity 
and Surveillance (OSIS) recommends accepting data without an on-site inspection on 06/12/2015 
as OSIS recently inspected these sites that conducted the two studies and the inspectional 
outcome from the inspections was classified as No Action Indicated (NAI).

Study Population: The study included healthy males and non-pregnant, non-lactating females 
ages between 18-55 with good health with a BMI between 18-30 kg/m2.  This study had 64 
healthy volunteers enrolled and 62 of them completed the study as planned by completing all four 
treatments.  Two subjects discontinued prematurely, one subject due to adverse event and one 
subject with voluntary withdrawal.  
Pharmacogenomic:  genotyping for CYP2C19 was conducted on Day 1 of period 1 for all 
subjects since dexlansoprazole is metabolized by the polymorphic isozyme CYP2C19.
Pharmacokinetic Measurements:
PK blood samples were collected at 0 hour (within 30 minutes before dosing) and 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 
4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 10, 12, 16, and 24 hours after dosing to determine the plasma concentration of 
dexlansoprazole on each period. 
PK Parameters Analysis:
Pharmacokinetic parameter estimates for dexlansoprazole were calculated using WinNonlin 
version 5.2 or higher with non-compartmental methods.  Statistical analysis was performed using 
ANOVA to compare the natural logarithmic (ln)-transformed PK parameters AUCs and Cmax of 
dexlansoprazole. The ANOVA model included sequence, period, and treatment as fixed effects, 
and subject within sequence as a random effect. Pairwise comparisons of each dexlansoprazole 
delayed-release OD tablet formulation to the dexlansoprazole delayed-release capsule were 
conducted and 90% CI for dexlansoprazole Cmax and AUCs central value ratios were computed. 
Bioequivalence (BE) between the regimens was concluded if the 90% CIs were completely 
contained within the equivalence range of 0.80 and 1.25 for dexlansoprazole Cmax and AUCs.
Actual sampling times, rather than scheduled sampling times, were used in all relevant 
computations. Concentrations below the lower limit of quantitation were set to zero for PK 
analysis.

Effect of Genotype on Dexlansoprazole Pharmacokinetics
Evaluation of CYP2C19 genotype indicated that 32 subjects were homozygous extensive 
metabolizers (EMs) (*1/*1), 7 subjects were heterozygous EMs (*1/*2 or *1/*4), 3 subjects were 
CYP2C19 homozygous (*17/*17) ultrarapid metabolizers (UMs),14 subjects were CYP2C19 
heterozygous (*1/*17 and *2/*17) UMs, and 3 subjects were homozygous poor metabolizer 
(*2/*2). No samples for genotype analysis were available for 4 subjects (Subjects 7001/015, 
7001/018, 7001/036, and 7001/085). One additional sample was obtained, but was never received 
by the lab (Subject 7001/074); No formal statistical analyses on dexlansoprazole PK were 
conducted based on CYP2C19 genotype. Metabolizer status was not expected to have an effect 
on the PD assessments or the determination of BA as this was a crossover study design and each 
subject acted as his or her own control.
Safety:
The safety endpoints evaluated in this study included physical examinations, vital signs, clinical 
laboratory test (haemotology, clinical chemistry, urinalysis,) 12-lead electrocardiogram (ECG), 
and adverse event (AE)  monitoring.   According to the sponsor, there was no death or serious 
adverse event (SAE) during the study. One subject (7001/045) experienced an adverse event of 
decreased neutrophils, absolute, on Study Day 7 that led to discontinuation of study drug. The 
investigator considered the event to be mild in severity and not related to study drug.
Of the 64 enrolled subjects, 10 (15.6%) experienced a TEAE during the study. The adverse events 
were primarily mild in intensity. The most common adverse event was vessel puncture site pain, 
which was experienced by 3 (4.7%) subjects.

concluded if the 90% CIs were completely contained within the equivalence range of (-12%, 
12%) for % time pH>4 over the 0 to 24-hour interval.

PK Parameters Analysis:
Pharmacokinetic parameter estimates for dexlansoprazole were calculated using Phoenix 
WinNonlin version 6.3.  Statistical analysis was performed using ANOVA to compare the natural 
logarithmic (ln)-transformed PK parameters AUCs and Cmax of dexlansoprazole. The ANOVA

random effect. Bioequivalence (BE) between the regimens was concluded if the 90% CIs were

and AUCs.

Effect of Genotype on Dexlansoprazole Pharmacokinetics
The CYP2C19 genotype was determined for 76 subjects (the analytical laboratory was unable to 
extract DNA from the blood sample for 1 subject 7001-120/1037). Of these, 28 subjects were 
homozygous extensive metabolizers (EMs, *1/*1), 14 subjects were heterozygous EMs (*1/*2 or 
*1/*9), 3 subjects were CYP2C19 homozygous ultrarapid metabolizers (UMs,*17/*17), 29 
subjects were CYP2C19 heterozygous (*1/*17 or *2/*17) UMs, and 2 subjects were CYP2C19 
homozygous PMs (*2/*2 or *3/*3).   No formal statistical analyses on dexlansoprazole PK were 
conducted based on CYP2C19 genotype. Metabolizer status was not expected to have an effect 
on the PD assessments or the determination of BA as this was a crossover study design and each 
subject acted as his or her own control.

occurrence of markedly abnormal laboratory values or changes in laboratory values reported as
