5. Nonclinical Pharmacology/Toxicology 
Executive Summary  
PIFELTRO is a once daily fixed-dose combination (FDC) tablet containing doravirine (DELSTRIGO; 
DOR), lamivudine (3TC) and tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF), and is intended for the 
treatment of HIV-1 infection in adults. DOR (also known as MK-1439) is a potent non-nucleoside 
reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI) that is being developed as a single entity to be part of an 
anti-retroviral therapy (ART), or as part of a fixed dose combination with 3TC and TDF. DOR is 
the only component of the FDC which has not been previously approved for use by the FDA. 
3TC and TDF have been approved for marketing in the U.S. as standalone agents (Epivir®; NDA 
020564 and Viread®; NDA 021356) and in multiple FDC products. 
 
The nonclinical safety profile of DOR was evaluated in: safety pharmacology studies in rats, 
mice and dogs; repeat-dose toxicology studies in mice, rats and dogs for up to 12, 26, and 39 
weeks, respectively; 12-week toxicology studies in rats to qualify impurities; phototoxicity 
studies in mouse fibroblasts and pigmented rats; juvenile, fertility and pre- and post-natal 
developmental studies in rats; embryo-fetal developmental studies in rats and rabbits; genetic 
toxicology studies (Ames, in vitro chromosomal aberration and in vivo rat micronucleus assays); 
and a 2-year carcinogenicity study in rats, as well as a 6-month study in transgenic mice. In 
addition, numerous in vitro and in vivo nonclinical pharmacokinetic studies evaluating the 
absorption, distribution, metabolism and excretion of DOR have been conducted in mice, rats, 
rabbits and dogs. Refer to the Pharmacology/Toxicology reviews for NDA-020564 and NDA-
021356 for a detailed summary of 3TC and TDF nonclinical data, respectively. 
 
Pharmacokinetics 
The steady state volume of distribution (Vss) following intravenous infusion of DOR was 
2.67 and 0.9 L/kg in rabbits and dogs with half-life (t½) values of 2.7, 6.4, 9, and 21.7 hr in mice, 
rats, rabbits, and dogs, respectively. DOR had low to moderate oral bioavailability (39%-47% in 
mice, rats, rabbits and dogs) with rapid absorption, reaching maximal plasma concentrations 
(Cmax) within 3.5 hours post-dose.  DOR had moderate binding to plasma protein (0.24) in all 
species examined and had wide tissue distribution in the rat, except the brain. The highest 
concentrations of radioactivity were observed in the alimentary canal, liver, and kidney, due to 
transit of the dose through the gastrointestinal tract and/or involvement in elimination. DOR 
did not display affinity for melanin.  
 
The equilibrium blood-to-plasma concentration ratio was independent of the DOR 
concentration and was approximately 1 across species, except in mice, for which the ratio 
varied from 0.65 to 0.71. As such, there was no preferential distribution of DOR into blood cells. 
DOR was eliminated primarily by oxidative metabolism in nonclinical species. The major

Reproductive Toxicology 
DOR exposure was not associated with effects on fertility.  Daily oral doses of DOR to

Developmental toxicity studies were conducted in rats and rabbits.  There were no adverse 
effects on maternal health or embryo-fetal development in rats, and the NOEL/NOAEL was 450 
and 300 mg/kg/day, for rats and rabbits, respectively. In rabbits, administration of oral DOR at 
300 mg/kg/day to pregnant females during organogenesis resulted in maternal toxicity (body 
weight loss) and concomitant fetal toxicity (skull malformations).  Due to maternal toxicity at 
this dose, the malformations were not considered test article-related. This finding occurred at 
exposures, based on AUC (315 μM·hr), approximately 8.5 times the projected clinical exposure 
of DOR following administration at the RHD.   
 
DOR maternal exposure was not associated with any adverse effects on pre- and postnatal 
development.  DOR at doses up to 450 mg/kg/day in pregnant rats had no maternal or fetal 
effects on behavior/development. The NOAEL was approximately 9 times higher than clinical 
exposure following DOR administration at the RHD. In addition, DOR had no clear adverse 
effects on rats dosed daily from post-natal day (PND) 14 to PND 55, in a juvenile toxicity study.  
The only significant finding was a dose-related increase in the incidence of MK-1439-treated 
rats with urine crystals (brown, needle-like, appearing in clusters) observed ex vivo following 
overnight urine collection in all test article-treated groups in post-natal week (PNW) 8 with 
increasing frequency at 300 mg/kg/day (highest dose and NOAEL); this finding was not 
correlated with any other clinical pathology observation or any anatomic pathology finding. The 
urine crystals were no longer present after a 4-week treatment-free period (PNW 13). The urine 
crystals were later determined to be formed ex vivo. The NOAEL of 300 mg/kg/day in the study 
was 9 times the clinical exposure of DOR following administration at the RHD. 
 
Other Toxicology 
DOR was nonphototoxic, and nonirritating to skin or eye.  DOR also showed no potential for 
sensitization.

Food Effect 
DOR and DOR/3TC/TDF may be dosed without regard to food. The effect of a standard high-fat 
meal on the PK of DOR from the oral 
 tablet (Study P037) and the combination 
DOR/3TC/TDF film-coated tablet (Study P029) was formally assessed in food effect trials in 
healthy subjects. Food increases DOR AUC0-inf and C24 by 16% and 36%, respectively, and did 
not remarkably change Cmax. Administration of DOR/3TC/TDF with food increased DOR AUC0-inf 
and C24 by 10% and 26%, respectively, but had no significant effect on Cmax. Increases to C24 
with food are likely due to slightly delayed Tmax. 3TC AUC0-inf and Cmax decreased by 7% and 
19% respectively, while tenofovir AUC0-inf increased by 27% and Cmax decreased by 12%. The 
magnitude of the food effect is not considered clinically significant for any of the three 
components. In the Phase 2/Phase 3 trials, DOR and DOR/3TC/TDF were dosed without regard 
to food. Therefore, DOR and DOR/3TC/TDF may be dosed without regard to food. 
 
Drug-Drug Interactions 
DOR as a victim drug: As shown from in vitro and in vivo studies, DOR is primarily metabolized 
by CYP3A, and drugs that induce or inhibit CYP3A may affect the clearance of DOR. Co-
administration of DOR and drugs that induce CYP3A result in decreased plasma concentrations 
of DOR. Co-administration of DOR and drugs that inhibit CYP3A result in increased plasma 
concentrations of DOR.

The modeling workflow for the DOR PBPK model is comprised of model building, model 
verification, and model application. Physicochemical and in vitro data, clinical IV and single oral

adequately captured the PK parameters of DOR. Simulated C24 values were also compared to

Doravirine Pharmacokinetics and Metabolism 
 
In vitro, doravirine displayed low intrinsic clearance with oxidation as the predominant hepatic 
metabolic pathway in preclinical species and humans with renal excretion as a secondary 
elimination pathway [17]. Using both recombinant enzymes and human liver microsomes with 
selective anti-CYP antibodies it was found that CYP3A4 plays a major role in doravirine’s 
metabolism [18]. Doravirine was not found to be a reversible inhibitor of major CYP enzymes 
(CYP1A2, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, and 3A4) or UGT1A1 at concentrations up to 100 µM [18]. 
Moreover, it was not a time-dependent inhibitor of CYP3A4 [18]. In human hepatocytes, 
doravirine was not an inducer of CYP1A2 or 2B6, with a weak induction of CYP3A4 (≥10 μM)

In cell lines, doravirine was a substrate, but not an inhibitor, of P-glycoprotein (P-gp) [18]. In vitro, 
doravirine inhibited OATP1B1, OATP1B3, OAT1, OAT3, OCT2, and BCRP with IC50 values of 
30, 39, 75, 16, 67, and 51 µM [18], respectively. However, due to the high IC50 values relative to 
its plasma Cmax of ~2 µM, it is not anticipated that doravirine would cause clinically relevant 
interactions with substrates of these transporters [18]. 
 
In clinical trials, doravirine has a Cmax of 1.74-2.88 μM and AUC0-∞ of 39.5-41.1 hr×µM [19, 20]. 
Drug-drug interaction studies have found that doravirine pharmacokinetics are not affected by 
dolutegravir or rifampin [21, 22]. Ritonavir, a CYP3A4 inhibitor and inducer, significantly 
increased doravirine AUC0-∞ and C24hr, with a more modest increase in Cmax [19]. Pretreatment 
with efavirenz, a CYP3A4 inducer, decreased doravirine’s AUC0-∞, and Cmax [20]. Doravirine 
slightly decreased the AUC0-∞, but not Cmax, of midazolam, a probe CYP3A4 substrate [23]. 
Although not clinically meaningful, atorvastatin Cmax was decreased by 33% while its AUC0-∞ was 
similar with and without doravirine [24].
