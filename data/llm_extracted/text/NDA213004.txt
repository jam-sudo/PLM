General Dosing and Therapeutic Individualization 
General Dosing 
The proposed RHB-105 dosing for the treatment of adults with H. pylori infection is four RHB­
105 capsules administered every eight hours with food for 14 days, equivalent to a total daily 
dose of 150 mg rifabutin, 3000 mg amoxicillin, and 120 mg omeprazole. 
Therapeutic Individualization 
CYP2C19 Genotype-inferred Phenotypes 
Omeprazole is extensively metabolized by the cytochrome P450 (CYP) enzymes, primarily by 
CYP2C19, which is a polymorphic enzyme. The systemic exposure to omeprazole varies with a 
patient’s metabolism status: poor metabolizers > intermediate metabolizers > normal 
metabolizers.  The Applicant proposes that RHB-105 may be administered regardless of 
CYP2C19 phenotypes. Safety, efficacy, and limited pharmacokinetic data from two Phase 3 
trials did not show appreciable differences in patients with various CYP2C19 phenotypes. Based 
on this information and the relatively short treatment duration (14 days), the Applicant’s 
proposal is acceptable. See sections 6.3.2, 17.3.3, and 17.3.4 for additional details.

Statistical Analysis Plan 
Analysis Populations 
The study had the following analysis populations: 
x Full Analysis Set (FAS) / Safety Population: all subjects who received at least one dose of 
randomized study treatment 
x Modified Intent-to-Treat (mITT) Population: all subjects who received at least one dose 
of randomized study treatment and underwent a 13C UBT test at Visit 5 (with the 
allowed exception for female women becoming pregnant and refusing the breath test) 
x  Per Protocol (PP) Population: all subjects who consumed at least 75% of planned study 
treatment received, underwent a 13C UBT test at Visit 5 (with the allowed exception for 
female women becoming pregnant and refusing the breath test), and had no major 
protocol violations that led to exclusion. 
x  Pharmacokinetic Population (PKP): subjects in the FAS that had demonstrable presence 
of any component of investigational drug at Visit 3 or for whom the PK assessment at 
Visit 3 was performed more than 250 hours after the last dose of randomized study drug 
before this assessment. 
Comment: FAS is also the Intent-to-treat (ITT) population. We used the ITT population as the 
primary efficacy analysis population. Exclusions based on information that occurred after 
randomization and treatment, such as undergoing a 13C UBT test at Visit 5, can lead to 
differences in treatment arms and difficulty in interpreting the trial results. 
The primary analysis was conducted using the FAS population. The analysis was repeated using 
the mITT and PP populations as sensitivity analyses.

Section 12.5 
CYP2C19, a polymorphic enzyme, is involved in the metabolism of omeprazole. The CYP2C19*1 
allele is fully functional while the CYP2C19*2 and *3 alleles are nonfunctional. There are other 
alleles associated with no or decreased CYP2C19 function.  Patients carrying two fully functional 
alleles are normal metabolizers and those carrying two nonfunctional alleles are poor 
metabolizers.  The systemic exposure to omeprazole varies with a patient’s metabolism status: 
poor metabolizers > intermediate metabolizers > normal metabolizers.  Approximately 3% of 
Caucasians and 15 to 20% of Asians are CYP2C19 poor metabolizers.  
In a pharmacokinetic study of a single 20 mg omeprazole dose, the AUC of omeprazole in Asian 
subjects was approximately four times higher than in Caucasians. In Study 1, the safety and 
tolerability of Talicia was not substantially different in CYP2C19 poor metabolizers (n = 5) and 
intermediate metabolizers (n = 48) compared to normal metabolizers (n = 114).
