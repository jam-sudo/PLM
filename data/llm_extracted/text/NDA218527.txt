Food effect 
assessment 
In the pivotal BA/BE Study TADA-22-051, the applicant 
assessed the effect of food on the BA of the proposed product. 
 
A high-fat, high-calorie meal was served 30 minutes prior to 
dosing for the fed condition. The GMRs (90% CIs) of fed to 
fasted conditions for Cmax [87.19% (80.01% – 95.02%)] and 
AUC0-∞ [111.63% (105.97% – 117.59%)] of tadalafil were all 
within the accept BE range of 80% to 125%. These results 
indicate that food intake does not have clinically meaningful 
impact on the PK of the proposed product. 
 
Water impact 
assessment 
The applicant conducted a dedicated randomized, single-dose, 
open-label, one-treatment, two-sequence, two-period, crossover 
study, TADA-23-023, to assess the PK of the proposed product, 
tadalafil chewable tablets 20 mg, when administered without the 
aid of water (treatment A) compared with that when administered 
with the aid of water (treatment B) in healthy male subjects, each 
under fasting conditions. 
 
The treatment A-to-treatment B GMRs (90% CIs) for Cmax 
[95.13% (88.9% – 101.81%)] and AUC0-∞ [98.63% (91.81% – 
105.96%)] of tadalafil were all within the acceptable BE range of 
80% to 125%. These results indicate that water intake during 
drug administration does not have a clinically meaningful impact 
on the PK of the proposed product.

Considering the above findings, the proposed drug tadalafil chewable tablets 20 mg and

established an adequate scientific bridge for reliance on the Agency’s previous findings 
of safety and efficacy for Cialis®. 
 
Reviewer’s comments: 
In the study described above (TADA-22-051), the mean half-life (t1/2) of tadalafil 
following administration of the proposed product and Cialis® (the listed drug) under 
fasting conditions was estimated to be approximately 35 hours and 36 hours, respectively 
(Table 3). These values are longer than the half-life of 17.5 hours presented in the 
approved labeling of Cialis®. The difference may be attributed to factors such as 
differences in the bioanalytical assay used for tadalafil concentration measurement, PK 
sampling timepoints used for half-life estimation, and/or study design (e.g., the 
characteristics of study subjects). Such a difference in half-life values based on a cross-
study comparison is not considered clinically meaningful considering that the BE criteria 
for AUC and Cmax were met and the half-life values of tadalafil were similar between 
the proposed product and Cialis® (the listed drug) in the same study.
